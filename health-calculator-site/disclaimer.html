Consult a Professional First

Before starting any new diet plan, please consult a qualified professional if you have: Diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, kidney disorders, eating disorders, thyroid conditions, gastrointestinal conditions, food allergies or intolerances, are pregnant or breastfeeding, are recovering from surgery or illness, or are on any prescribed medication.

The meal plans generated by NutritionTracker are algorithmic outputs based on standard nutritional guidelines (ICMR-NIN, IFCT 2017). They are not reviewed by a healthcare professional on a per-user basis.

We strongly encourage all users to share their generated plan with a registered dietitian or doctor before following it, particularly if they have any underlying health concerns.

Accuracy of Content

We make reasonable efforts to ensure the accuracy of the nutritional information, calorie estimates, and meal recommendations on NutritionTracker. Our primary data sources include:

  • Indian Food Composition Tables (IFCT 2017) by NIN, Hyderabad
  • ICMR Recommended Dietary Allowances (RDA) for Indians
  • National Institute of Nutrition (NIN) dietary guidelines

However, nutritional values can vary significantly based on ingredient quality, regional variation, cooking method, portion size, and preparation technique. The values presented should be treated as estimates, not precise measurements.

NutritionTracker does not guarantee the completeness, accuracy, or timeliness of any nutritional information and reserves the right to update content without notice.

Nutrition and weight management are highly individualised. Factors including genetics, metabolism, gut microbiome, sleep quality, stress levels, hormonal status, physical activity, and adherence all influence outcomes.

NutritionTracker does not guarantee any specific results — including but not limited to weight loss, muscle gain, improved energy, or any other health outcome. Statements such as "typical results" or user experiences shared as testimonials represent individual cases and may not reflect your personal experience.

Tip: Treat your NutritionTracker meal plan as a starting point, not a rigid prescription. Adjust portion sizes and food choices based on your own hunger, energy, and preferences.
05

Nutritional Data & Food Database

The nutritional values available in our Food Database are derived from IFCT 2017 and supplementary Indian food databases. These are provided for informational reference only.

Nutrient content in real food can deviate from database values due to:

  • Seasonal and regional variation in produce
  • Cooking methods (boiling, frying, steaming) affecting nutrient retention
  • Brand-specific differences in packaged foods
  • Serving size estimation inaccuracies

Users requiring precise nutritional analysis (e.g. for clinical or athletic purposes) should use laboratory-tested values or consult a sports dietitian.

Health Calculators & Tools

The BMI, TDEE, BMR, ideal weight, and other calculators available on NutritionTracker use standard formulae (Mifflin-St Jeor, Harris-Benedict, etc.) validated for general adult populations. These tools provide estimates based on population averages and may not accurately reflect your individual physiology.

In particular:

  • BMI does not account for muscle mass, bone density, or fat distribution — it is a screening tool, not a diagnostic one.
  • TDEE estimates assume standard activity multipliers and may not reflect your actual energy expenditure.
  • Calorie targets generated are starting estimates and should be adjusted based on real-world progress.
