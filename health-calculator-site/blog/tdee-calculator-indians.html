TDEE for Indians: Calculate Your Daily Calorie Need Based on Lifestyle

Office worker, construction worker, homemaker — your calorie needs vary enormously. Here is how to calculate an accurate TDEE using India-specific activity multipliers.

👤Priya Sharma
📅17 June 2025
🔄Updated 30 June 2025
⏱ 5 min read

📌 Key Takeaways

  • TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is the total calories your body burns in a day
  • The standard Western activity multipliers underestimate calorie needs for manual labour jobs common in India
  • BMR is the baseline — TDEE = BMR × activity factor
  • To lose weight: eat 300–500 kcal below TDEE. To gain: eat 250–400 kcal above TDEE
  • TDEE changes as you lose weight — recalculate every 4–6 weeks

What Is TDEE and Why Does It Matter?

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is the total number of calories your body burns in a 24-hour period, accounting for all activity — from your base metabolic functions to exercise to the energy cost of digesting food. It is the single most important number for anyone trying to manage their weight.

TDEE comprises four components:
  • BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate): Calories burned at complete rest — typically 60–75% of TDEE
  • NEAT (Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis): Calories from daily movement outside exercise — walking, cooking, cleaning
  • EAT (Exercise Activity Thermogenesis): Calories from structured exercise
  • TEF (Thermic Effect of Food): Calories burned digesting food — roughly 10% of total calories consumed

How to Calculate Your BMR

The Mifflin-St Jeor equation is the most accurate BMR formula for most adults:

📐

Men: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) − (5 × age) + 5
Women: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) − (5 × age) − 161

Woman, 30y, 60kg, 160cm(600)+(1000)−(150)−1611,289 kcal
Man, 35y, 75kg, 172cm(750)+(1075)−(175)+51,655 kcal
Woman, 45y, 70kg, 158cm(700)+(987.5)−(225)−1611,301 kcal

India-Specific Activity Multipliers

Standard activity multipliers were developed from Western population data. Here are adjusted multipliers that better reflect common Indian occupational activity levels:

Activity LevelExample Indian OccupationsMultiplier
SedentaryIT professional, call centre, WFH desk job× 1.2
Lightly activeTeacher, shop owner, homemaker (urban)× 1.375
Moderately activeSales executive (field), nurse, small business owner× 1.55
Very activeFarmer, construction worker, delivery person× 1.725
Extremely activeManual labourer, athlete in training, army soldier× 1.9
TDEE = BMR × Activity Multiplier. A 35-year-old man with a BMR of 1,655 kcal in a desk job: TDEE = 1,655 × 1.2 = 1,986 kcal/day. To lose weight, target 1,486–1,686 kcal/day.

Adjusting Your TDEE Over Time

TDEE is not a fixed number — it changes as your body composition changes. This is why weight loss plateaus occur:

  • As you lose weight, your BMR decreases because you are carrying less body mass. Recalculate TDEE every 4–6 weeks or after every 3–4 kg of weight loss.
  • As you gain muscle, your BMR increases slightly — this is one reason strength training is recommended alongside calorie restriction.
  • Metabolic adaptation: After 12+ weeks of consistent calorie restriction, the body adapts by reducing NEAT — unconsciously moving less. Build in a maintenance week (eating at TDEE) every 8–10 weeks to reset.
